Ohio Bingo Regulations - Detailed Reference

Key Summary

Licensing Authority
State (Attorney General)
Prize Limit
$6,000 per session
Session Frequency
3 per 7 days
Minimum Age
18 years old

Licensing & Registration

  • Authority: Ohio Attorney General's Office - Charitable Law Section
  • License Type: Charitable organization license required
  • Application Process: Submit application to AG's office with organizational documents
  • License Duration: Annual renewal required
  • Fees: License fees vary by organization type

Prize & Payout Rules

  • Maximum Prize: $6,000 total per session across all games
  • Individual Game Prize: No individual game limit, but session total cannot exceed $6,000
  • Payout Calculation: Prize limits strictly enforced by state monitoring
  • Special Games: Coverall, progressive, and bonanza games count toward $6,000 limit

Session & Operational Rules

  • Session Frequency: Maximum 3 sessions per 7-day period
  • Session Duration: No specific time limit, but must be reasonable
  • Timing Restrictions: Sessions must be spaced appropriately within the 7-day period
  • Location: Must be at licensed premises
  • Operators: Volunteer-based; professional operators allowed with restrictions

Financial & Reporting

  • Record Keeping: Detailed records of all sessions, receipts, and disbursements required
  • Quarterly Reports: Financial reports must be filed with AG's office
  • Annual Reports: Comprehensive annual financial disclosure required
  • Audit Requirements: Subject to AG audit at any time
  • Use of Proceeds: Funds must be used for charitable purposes as defined by state law

Compliance & Enforcement

  • Centralized System: Ohio operates a state-wide centralized monitoring system
  • State Oversight: AG's office has broad enforcement powers
  • Violations: License suspension or revocation for non-compliance
  • Penalties: Fines and criminal penalties for serious violations

Key Features

  • Centralized State System: All bingo activity monitored at state level
  • Strict Enforcement: AG's office actively enforces regulations
  • Consistent Standards: Same rules apply statewide (no local variations)
  • Charitable Focus: Strong emphasis on charitable use of proceeds

Resources

  • Primary Authority: Ohio Revised Code Chapter 2915.07-2915.13
  • Contact: Ohio Attorney General's Office, Charitable Law Section
  • Website: www.ohioattorneygeneral.gov
California Bingo Regulations - Detailed Reference

Key Summary

Licensing Authority
Local (City/County)
Prize Limit
$500 (state) / varies locally
Session Frequency
Varies by jurisdiction
Minimum Age
18 years old

Licensing & Registration

  • Authority: City or County where bingo is conducted
  • Dual Licensing: Requires both state and local approval
  • State Registration: File with California Attorney General (Registry of Charitable Trusts)
  • Local License: City council or county board of supervisors approval required
  • License Duration: Annual renewal (dates vary by jurisdiction)
  • Application Process: Submit to local jurisdiction with organizational documents

Prize & Payout Rules

  • State Maximum: $250 per game as baseline
  • Local Override: Cities/counties may set higher limits (common range: $500-$2,500)
  • Santa Clara Example: $500 per game maximum in most Santa Clara County jurisdictions
  • Prize Pool: Some jurisdictions allow progressive jackpots with separate rules
  • Special Games: Coveralls and bonanzas may have higher limits depending on local ordinance

Session & Operational Rules

  • Session Frequency: Set by local ordinance (typically 1-7 sessions per week)
  • Session Hours: Local restrictions apply (often limited to specific times/days)
  • Location Requirements: Must be at facility owned/leased by licensed organization
  • Operators: Volunteer-only in some jurisdictions; paid staff allowed in others
  • Combined Events: Some localities allow bingo with other fundraising activities

Financial & Reporting

  • State Reporting: Annual financial report (Form RRF-1) to AG's office
  • Local Reporting: Varies by jurisdiction (monthly, quarterly, or annual)
  • Record Keeping: Detailed records of receipts, prizes, and expenses required
  • Use of Proceeds: At least 90% of net proceeds must go to charitable purposes
  • Expense Limits: Administrative and rental expenses capped (varies by jurisdiction)
  • Audit Requirements: May be subject to AG or local jurisdiction audit

Compliance & Enforcement

  • Decentralized System: Each jurisdiction enforces its own ordinances
  • State Oversight: AG's office monitors charitable trust compliance
  • Local Enforcement: City/county officials conduct inspections
  • Violations: License suspension, revocation, or criminal penalties
  • Variance: Rules can differ significantly between neighboring jurisdictions

Key Features

  • Decentralized Local Control: Each city/county sets its own detailed rules
  • Variable Standards: Prize limits, session frequency, and operational rules vary widely
  • Two-Tier System: Must comply with both state law and local ordinances
  • Home Rule: Cities have broad authority to regulate bingo within their boundaries
  • Charitable Focus: Strong emphasis on charitable organization ownership and control

County-Specific Notes

  • Santa Clara County: Most cities allow $500 per game; sessions vary by city
  • San Mateo County: Similar to Santa Clara with local variations
  • Los Angeles County: Wide variation across numerous cities
  • San Diego County: Generally more restrictive prize limits

Resources

  • Primary Authority: California Penal Code Sections 326.5, 326
  • State Contact: California Attorney General - Registry of Charitable Trusts
  • Local Contact: City Clerk or County Clerk where bingo is conducted
  • Website: oag.ca.gov/charities
